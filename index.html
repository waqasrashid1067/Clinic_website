Technique 01

FUE

Follicles are taken one at a time from the back of the scalp with a rotary punch, leaving dots rather than a line. No stitches, no scalpel scar.

  • Punch sizes from 0.7–0.9 mm
  • Recipient channels opened by hand
  • Grafts sorted under a microscope
  • Suits large areas and high graft counts

See pricing →

Technique 02

DHI

The graft is loaded into an implanter pen, so the channel is opened and filled in one movement. Less time out of the body for each follicle.

  • Choi-type implanter pens
  • Denser packing per square centimetre
  • Works between existing hairs
  • Best for hairlines and small zones

Instruments

The tools on the tray,
and where they come from.

Most clinics will not tell you what they are using. Here is our full set. Every sterile-contact item is single-use and opened in front of you.

Choi implanter pens

Hollow-needle pens in 0.8, 0.9 and 1.0 mm gauges for DHI placement. Needles are single-use.

Origin: China · single-use tips

Sapphire blades

Crystal-tipped blades for opening recipient channels. They make a V-shaped cut that closes tighter than a steel slit.

Origin: China · single-use

Micromotor punch system

Variable-speed rotary handpiece with 0.7–0.9 mm punches, chosen to match the thickness of your own follicles.

Origin: China · sterilised per case

Magnification & graft storage

Surgical loupes for placement, a stereo microscope for sorting, and chilled holding solution so grafts spend as little time out as possible.

How much does a hair transplant cost in Islamabad?

It depends on how many grafts you need, and that is decided at the consultation rather than over the phone. Every patient gets a written quotation with one total before anything is booked, and that total does not change afterwards. Starting figures are on the prices page.

Does a hair transplant hurt?

The scalp is fully numbed with local anaesthetic, so the procedure itself is not painful. Most people describe the following two days as tightness rather than pain, and ordinary painkillers are enough.

How long until it looks normal?

Redness settles within about two weeks. Transplanted hairs shed around week three, which is expected and not a failure. Growth starts around month four, and the final result is judged at twelve months.

What is the difference between FUE and DHI?

Both take follicles out one at a time. In FUE the channels are opened first and grafts placed after. In DHI the graft sits inside an implanter pen and the channel is opened and filled in one movement, which suits dense packing and working between hairs you still have.

Can I have an online consultation first?

Yes. Video consultations are available if you are outside Islamabad or overseas. Send clear photographs of your scalp beforehand and you will get an honest answer on whether the trip is worth making. Book one on the contact page.

Can I pay in instalments?

Yes, for larger treatments. Ask at the consultation and the schedule goes into your written plan rather than being agreed verbally.

Why do procedures start in the evening?

A full session runs six to eight hours. Starting at 6 pm means it is never cut short to make room for the next appointment, and most patients prefer taking one day off rather than a morning.
